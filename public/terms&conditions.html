Declan

I'm destined to become the next CEO of my family's media empire.
The only problem? My grandfather's inheritance clause.
Fulfilling his dying wish of getting married and having an heir seemed impossible until my assistant volunteered for the job.
Our marriage was supposed to be the perfect solution to my biggest problem.
But the more we act in love for the public, the more unsure I feel about our contract.
Caring about Iris was never part of the deal.
Especially not when breaking her heart is inevitable.

Iris

My plan to marry Declan was simple in theory.
Move in together. Throw a wedding. Have a baby.
We set rules to prevent any kind of issues.
Ones that were never meant to be broken, no matter how much Declan tempts me.
But what happens when our fake relationship bleeds into our real one?
Falling in love was never an option.
At least not for me.

10/11
  A 4.5 read with an impressive writing style!(No SPOILERS)
Reviewed in India on 11 June 2023
Verified purchase

The book is about the second Kane brother, Declan, who, according to his grandfather's will, has to marry and have kids in order to become the next CEO of Dreamland. The engagement is arranged with a girl but apparently she quits all of a sudden leaving our heroine, Iris, to enter the story and marry Declan.

This book is primarily grumpy x sunshine type where Iris is the sunshine, optimistic and loves her life despite her childhood and present conditions. Declan is the grumpy type where he doesn't care about anyone's emotions, does his work like a machine, short-tempered type. It's a fake marriage and finally turns into a real one. The story line seems casual, nothing new but the progress in the story, the flaws in the main characters, the dialogues.... It was absolutely amazing, and mostly, very original. It doesn't feel like I'm reading a book but actually reading a real story like it happened in someone's life. The gradual change in Declan's character is slow but noticeable and that's great! He was the most flawed character in the book but he stole my heart. The ending of the book is also a good asset. Only the over romance at times was not my cup of tea and I had to skip few pages.
